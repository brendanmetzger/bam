The cook lay stuck, a wound weeping crimson, his breath quick and difficult. He pauses to wince; a rolling popple bellows into the weathered deck of the faithful brig; flatus. The knell of a desperate voyage and a galley well-nigh bare of all but legumes. Grinning, the cook looks to his companion, eyes glistening. Gravity eases for a moment as the captain shudders with unwelcome laughter. He recovers, allowing his gaze to return to his old friend. The cook is dead.
